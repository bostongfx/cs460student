RO.ME "3 DREAMS OF BLACK"

RO.ME: 3 Dreams of Black (2011) is a pioneering interactive music film that ran natively in the browser using early WebGL and Three.js. It blended real-time 3D scenes, animation, and synchronized audio into a navigable story—no plugins required. The project proved that cinematic, interactive graphics could live on the open web and helped kick-start a wave of Three.js/WebGL experiments. Its influence is visible in today immersive web experiences for art, data storytelling, and branded interactives.

Technologies used:
WebGL (hardware-accelerated graphics in the browser)
Three.js (high-level 3D framework on top of WebGL)
HTML5 / CSS3 / JavaScript (structure, layout, interaction)
GLSL shaders (materials, lighting, post-effects)
Web Audio + timing synchronization for interactive sequences

Blob Mixer (14islands) showcases modern WebGL/Three.js craft—smooth micro-interactions, signed-distance “blob” modeling, and polished post-processing. Compared with RO.ME long-form, music-driven narrative from 2011, Blob Mixer reflects today playful, tool-like experiences and physically based rendering polish. Both demonstrate the power of real-time 3D on the open web—just from different eras and goals.
