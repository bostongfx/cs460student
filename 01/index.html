RO.ME "3 DREAMS OF BLACK"

RO.ME: "3 Dreams of Black" (2011) is an interactive video that ran completely in a web browser without any plugins. It used 3d scenes, and camera movement to show that the open web could also handle interactive graphics like that. In 2011, most 3D videos on the web used flash. RO.ME showed that WebGL could create stories and complex visuals on a browser itself.

Technology: HTML5, Javascript, WebGL, Google Chrome, Three.js

Blob Mixer - Creating and sharing 3D blobs from Comparison: RO.ME is like an early WebGL music video where the camera moves on its own and there are visuals to connect everything. Blob Mixer is very different becasue it lets you play around by changing the 3D blob. You can change the shape to however you want. The blob mixer is experimental while RO.ME is more about watching an artistic movie.

Significance (AI): According to ChatGPT, RO.ME was a milestone because it proved that WebGL and HTML5 could deliver cinematic 3D graphics right in the browser without plugins. It inspired future interactive art and set the stage for modern web experiences. - ChatGPT
