RO.ME "3 DREAMS OF BLACK"

The RO.ME demo was a pretty cool experience I got to interact with. Several types of scenes were displayed such as video, 2D, and 3d scenes. I was able to move my cursor around which generated a variety of animals and geometric shapes. My favorite part was the 3d scene at the end where you fly with the pack of birds. Looking at the behind the scenes video, I was surprised to see it was 14 years ago that the movie was made. I wonder how people from back then reacted to this movie.

Technology: WebGL (JavaScript and HTML), a supported browser, a video card.

Comparison: Quantum - this website is for a packaging company (a concept as I can tell). Both this and RO.ME have an interactive element that the user can do. Quantum has the user scroll forward or backwards to view their "Quantum packaging" features, using hundreds of spaced dots to simulate shapes much like a synchronized drone show. RO.ME allows the user to move their mouse to draw/ generate a multitude of animals and patterns. Both very cool graphics.

Significance (AI): My question to ChatGPT was "why was ro.me (interactive webgl demo) so important?" Summarizing its response, it was important because it showcased that modern browsers were capable of rendering 3d graphics in real time. There was no need for special hardware, anyone using a supported browser can view it. Along with the technical aspects, it showed how the web can be an artistic medium for artists, increasing creative output.
